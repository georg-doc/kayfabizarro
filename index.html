Put on your mask. Let's wrestle. — Uncle FrizzleBob, Professional Reality-Wrestler & Epistemiologic Outlaw
What's the game?

A collaborative storytelling card game where every card is a character waiting to be performed. You roll a die for your story mode, introduce a card with a theatrical ritual, tell a 1–3 sentence story in character, and push a shared Quest Die toward the Finale. No board. No score. No winner. Just a story that didn't exist before you sat down — and a deep suspicion that reality was negotiable all along.

Two flavours. Same bones. Choose your reality:

Original
Kayfabizarro Freestyle
Underground zine aesthetic. Six story modes from Tragic to Forbidden. For anyone who's ever wanted to narrate a toaster's legal career. 1–6 players · 20–60 min.
Clinical Edition
MedKayfab
Mechanisms, not memorisation. Physiology wrestles pathology. Active recall built into every turn. For medical students, educators & epistemic outlaws. Solo or 1–6.

MEDKAYFAB — Mnemonic Medicine Mayhem

The Clinical Story Wrestling Card Game · Solo or 1–6 · 20–60 Min · CC BY-NC-SA 4.0
"This is not a game you play. This is a reality you negotiate, one card at a time, until somebody flinches. Nobody wins. Nobody loses. Everybody leaves slightly changed. That's the plan. Stay fluffy."
"Forget rote memorization and mindless lists. We are here to wrestle with physiology, outwit the pathogen, and diagnose the system — the biological one and the broken one. Stay fluffy — and always read the primary literature."
What is this?

You tell a shared story using cards, two dice, and whatever's in your head. One sentence at a time. Everyone takes turns introducing a card and building on what came before, until the Quest Die hits 6 and the story lands.

Kayfabe
Wrestling-speak for "the fiction is real." Your story is the reality. Commit to it — no winking at the camera.
Bizarro
The permission to be strange and illogical. A toaster can be your lawyer. Logic is optional. Surprises are mandatory.
"No win condition. No score. Just a story that didn't exist before you sat down — and a sense of whose stories landed. Most people still ask if there's a winner. There isn't. You are the winner. Congratulations."

What you need

Cards: Each player brings 15–20 illustrated cards. Shuffle into one shared deck. Every card can be a Character, Scene, or Quest.
Two D6: One Story-Mode Die, one Quest Die. Both stay visible at all times.
1–6 People: And a table. No board. No pawns. No manual thicker than your wrist.

You each play a Character — a clinician, a drug, an immune cell, a rogue signalling molecule, or a personified disease. Together you build a shared Clinical Stage and push a Medical Quest toward resolution.

MedKayfab
"The pathophysiology is real." We treat biological mechanisms like living wrestlers. If you understand the mechanism, you win the match against forgetting.
Bizarro-Medicine
The permission to sabotage outdated textbooks and let absurdity carry the mechanism home. Blödsinn! rules — but only after peer review.
"No board. No pawns. No 800-page manual. That was a design decision. The complexity is in the cards — and in the mechanism."

What you need

MedKayfab Cards: From any deck (Cardiology, Neurology, Immunology, etc.). Mix freely — the human body doesn't respect specialty boundaries.
Two D6: One Story-Mode Die, one Quest Die. Both visible at all times.
Optional: A blank card or notebook page for the Mechanism Log.

The Intro Ritual

Name It
Point at the card. Announce its name loud.
Claim It
One sentence: what does this card want? Read the LORE or invent from the image.
Power It
Don't explain it. Perform it. Show us what this Power does to reality right now.
The Intro Ritual applies to every card, every time. The story starts the moment you commit.

The Stage — 3 Scene Slots

Cards ARE the location. "Burning Trashcan" means you're standing next to one. Stage full (3/3)? Overwrite a slot with a yes-and — choose one to discard.

Character Switch

Play a Character card into your own slot at any time. Old character leaves or becomes an NPC. Change your voice and posture immediately.
"A good death scene is a gift to the story. Accept gifts."

Moving the Die

+2 — Hard push. Chaos incoming.
+1 — Normal progression.
0 — Hold. Linger. Build character.
–1 — Step back. When a twist reframes everything.
Quest Die never goes below 1. At 6: Finale triggered next turn.

The Finale

Memory: Quest card stays as shared record.
Clear: Stage cleared. All Scene cards to discard.
New Quest: Next player runs Quest Reveal ritual.
Reset: Quest Die back to 1. New episode begins.
"The Finale doesn't have to be good. It has to be true — true to what everyone built. A messy ending that was earned beats a neat ending that was imported."

Optional Rules

Quest Fail: Die below 1 → Quest fails. No Finale. The collapse is the story.
Staged Clearing: Roll after Finale. 1–2: remove 1 Scene. 3–4: remove 2. 5–6: all three.
Social Calls

After a story — before the next player starts. Quest Die move stands unless Blödsinn! lands.

KayfaBINGO!
That landed.
Pure praise. No explanation needed. Storyteller draws an extra card.
KayfaBOGGLE?
I'm lost.
One concrete question — not a judgment. Active player answers in 1–2 sentences, in character.
KayfaBONGO!
Power as mechanic.
Someone resolved a Power like a boardgame — call it. They restate as story, not transaction.
BLÖDSINN!
Broke Kayfabe.
Rule broken: out of character, Scene forgotten, Mode ignored. Majority decides. If stands: Quest Die does not move.
"King Kayfabian (Optional): Final call on disputes. Passes left after one story. No permanent power. Most tables won't need this. Most tables."

Table Culture — Non-Negotiable

Kayfab-Finger Rule: Point at every card as you mention it.
Silence Is Allowed: 10 seconds to think. Don't rush the Bizarro.
Overacting Is Encouraged: If you're not embarrassed, you're not playing hard enough.
X-Card: Tap the table to stop and reset a scene. No questions asked.
Laughter Breaks Nothing: If everyone's laughing, you're playing it right.

Solo Play — The Lonely Rabbit Mode

Play all Characters yourself. Switch seats or hats. After each story bit, write one sentence about what shifted.
"I'm my own best audience anyway."

Solo Play — Study Session Mode

Draw 5 cards. Pick one as Character. Draw a Quest or write your own. Stage empty. Quest Die to 1. Play all Characters yourself. After each story: write one sentence in the Mechanism Log.
"Solo MedKayfab is the Feynman Technique with cards and a die. The cards make the gaps visible."
Powers & Cards
Nothing on a card is a rule.

Everything on a card is an invitation to perform.

Anatomy of a Card

Image — Makes a claim without words.
Lore — Context or backstory. Use it or invent from the image.
Power — What this card does in the story. Mandatory when introduced. Takes priority.

How to Use a Power

Don't say:
"I deal 3 damage to you."
Say:
"Your empire glitches. The generals forget what war is. Seven kinds of collapse happen at once."
"Powers are the only way to legally cheat. Use them. If a Power is boring, make it weird."

The Mechanism Log

After each Quest Finale, one player writes — in one sentence — the core mechanism that emerged. At the end of a session, read the Log aloud. Every mechanism you can name: that's the learning that survived the game.
Learning Add-Ons

Optional layers for when the group wants to study harder, not just play.

Add-On A — Anki Mode

After each Finale: Front = character name + clinical clue. Back = core mechanism in one sentence. Add to Anki. Review tomorrow.
"MedKayfab surfaces the card. Anki makes it stick. Same memory cycle, different timescales."

Add-On B — Feynman Round

Once per Quest, one player explains a card's mechanism to the group with no jargon, one minute max. Group understands it: Quest Die +1 bonus.
"If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet. Feynman said that. Doc FrizzleBob agrees — reluctantly."

Add-On C — Spaced Repetition

Shuffle in 3–5 marked cards from previous sessions. When one appears: explain from memory before Intro Ritual. Success: +1 Quest Die. Failure: card still plays, gap logged.

Add-On D — Peer Teaching Round

After each Finale, roll D6 — highest = teacher. 3 minutes, one card, mechanism + clinical relevance. Group asks one question each.
"The best way to learn something is to teach it. The best way to teach it is to not be afraid of the gap."

FAQ

Do I need to mention every Scene card on the Stage?

Yes — at least acknowledge each one. A word is enough. Recognition, not analysis.

What if my story doesn't make sense?

That's fine. Plausible is better than correct. The act of trying to make it plausible is the point. If it's truly broken, KayfaBOGGLE will surface it.

Can I invent Powers / mechanisms?

Yes. Cards don't enforce rules. They enforce claims. Invented mechanisms that are wrong will eventually break the story. That break is the learning.

Can I pass?

Yes. Use it sparingly — passing kills momentum.

Is there a correct way to play?

No. Only the rules that emerge at your table. The game adapts to the group — not the other way around.

Can I mix Kayfabizarro and MedKayfab decks?

Absolutely. A Cardiology card, a Germanic Tribes card, and a Protopia card on the same Stage is exactly the kind of chaos the game was designed for.

About

Kayfabizarro and MedKayfab are created by Georg Graf von Westphalen — game designer, cartoonist, and epistemic outlaw based in Cologne, Germany.

The games grew out of his satirical comic Hilfe, ich bin ein Mensch and the design principle of Übererfüllung — overfulfilment of a system's internal logic until the system reveals itself through the completeness of its own description.
