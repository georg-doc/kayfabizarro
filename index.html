KAYFABIZARRO
FREESTYLE

The Social Story-Wrestling Card Game · 1–6 Players · 20–60 Min · CC BY-NC-SA 4.0
"This is not a game you play. This is a reality you negotiate, one card at a time, until somebody flinches. Nobody wins. Nobody loses. Everybody leaves slightly changed. That's the plan. Stay fluffy."
What is this?

You tell a shared story using cards, two dice, and whatever's in your head. One sentence at a time. Everyone takes turns introducing a card and building on what came before, until the Quest Die hits 6 and the story lands.

Read this section and Setup, then start. The rest makes sense as you play.

Kayfabe
Wrestling-speak for "the fiction is real." Your story is the reality. Commit to it — no winking at the camera. If you say there's an invisible elephant in the room, everyone can smell it.
Bizarro
The permission to be strange and illogical. A toaster can be your lawyer. Logic is optional. Surprises are mandatory. Don't explain it — perform it.
"No win condition. No score. Just a story that didn't exist before you sat down — and a sense of whose stories landed. That's the whole game. Most people still ask if there's a winner. There isn't. You are the winner. Congratulations."

What you need

Cards: Each player brings 15–20 illustrated cards — tarot, cut-up comics, playing cards with images. Shuffle everything into one shared face-down deck. Every card can be a Character, Scene, or Quest.
Two D6: One Story-Mode Die, one Quest Die. Both stay visible at all times.
1–6 People: And a table. No board. No pawns. No manual thicker than your wrist.

The Intro Ritual — Name It / Claim It / Power It

Every card that enters the game must be introduced via the Ritual. Think: wrestling entrance. Three beats. Thirty seconds. In character.

Name It
Point at the card. Announce its name loud. This is who or what just entered.
Claim It
One sentence: what does this card want? Read it off the card or invent it from the image.
Power It
Don't explain it. Perform it. Show us what this Power does to reality right now.
The Intro Ritual applies to every card, every time — Quest cards, Scene cards, new Characters. Always. The story starts the moment you commit.

The Stage — 3 Scene Slots

Cards ARE the location: "Burning Trashcan" means you're standing next to one. The Stage is the shared reality.
Stage full (3/3)? Overwrite a slot with a yes-and. Choose one to discard. Make it dramatic.

Character Switch

Play a Character card into your own slot at any time. Old character leaves or becomes an NPC. Change your voice and posture immediately.
"A good death scene is a gift to the story. Accept gifts."

A story compass, not a countdown. Not a timer. You move it. You decide when to push hard or let things breathe.

Moving the Die

+2 — Hard push. Chaos incoming. Use sparingly.
+1 — Normal progression.
0 — Hold. Linger. Build character.
–1 — Step back. When a twist reframes everything.
Quest Die never goes below 1. At 6: Finale triggered next turn.

The Finale

Memory: Quest card stays on table as shared record.
Clear: Stage cleared. All Scene cards to discard.
New Quest: Next player runs full Quest Reveal ritual.
Reset: Quest Die back to 1. New episode begins.
"The Finale doesn't have to be good. It has to be true — true to what everyone built. A messy ending that was earned beats a neat ending that was imported."

Optional Rules

Quest Fail: Die below 1 → Quest fails. No Finale, no memory. The collapse is the story.
Staged Clearing: Roll after Finale. 1–2: remove 1 Scene. 3–4: remove 2. 5–6: all three. Survivors carry history forward.
Social Calls

After a story — before the next player starts — anyone can call one of these. Quest Die move stands unless Blödsinn! lands.

KayfaBINGO!
That landed.
Pure praise. No explanation needed. Storyteller draws an extra card. Shout it. Mean it.
KayfaBOGGLE?
I'm lost.
One concrete question — not a judgment. Active player answers in 1–2 sentences, in character. Play continues.
KayfaBONGO!
Power as mechanic.
Someone resolved a Power like a boardgame — call it. They restate as story, not transaction.
BLÖDSINN!
Broke Kayfabe.
Rule broken: out of character, Scene forgotten, Mode ignored. Majority decides. If stands: Quest Die does not move.
"King Kayfabian (Optional): Final call on disputes. Passes left after one story. No permanent power. Most tables won't need this. Most tables."

Table Culture — Non-Negotiable

Kayfab-Finger Rule: Point at every card as you mention it. Makes you look slightly unhinged. That's the point.
Silence Is Allowed: 10 seconds to think. Don't rush the Bizarro.
Overacting Is Encouraged: If you're not embarrassed, you're not playing hard enough.
X-Card: Tap the table to stop and reset a scene. No questions asked.
Laughter Breaks Nothing: If everyone's laughing, you're playing it right.

Solo Play — The Lonely Rabbit Mode

Play all Characters yourself. Switch seats or hats. After each story bit, write one sentence about what shifted — this replaces Social Calls.
"I'm my own best audience anyway."

Anatomy of a Card

Image — Makes a claim without words. Starting point for any story.
Quote — Gives the card a voice. Anchor for your narration.
Lore — Context or backstory. Use it or ignore it. The "Why."
Power — What this card does in the story. Mandatory when introduced. Takes priority.

How to Use a Power

Powers are narrative — not mechanics, not damage counters. Read the Power aloud in the Intro Ritual, then show us what it looks like when it comes alive.

Don't say:
"I deal 3 damage to you."
Say:
"Your empire glitches. The generals forget what war is. Seven kinds of collapse happen at once."
"Powers are the only way to legally cheat. Use them. If a Power is boring, make it weird. That's not a rule — that's just good advice."

MEDKAYFAB
MNEMONIC MEDICINE MAYHEM

The Clinical Story Wrestling Card Game · Solo or 1–6 Students/Outlaws · 20–60 Min · CC BY-NC-SA 4.0
"Forget rote memorization and mindless lists. We are here to wrestle with physiology, outwit the pathogen, and diagnose the system — the biological one and the broken one. One sentence at a time, until the case resolves, the patient survives, or the mechanism finally makes sense. Stay fluffy — and always read the primary literature."
What is this? Clinical Edition

You each play a Character — a clinician, a drug, an immune cell, a rogue signalling molecule, or a personified disease. Together you build a shared Clinical Stage and push a Medical Quest toward resolution.

When the Quest Die reaches 6, someone tells the Finale — the diagnosis is made, the mechanism is revealed, the patient survives (or doesn't). No win condition. No score. Just a mechanism you won't forget.

MedKayfab
"The pathophysiology is real." We treat biological mechanisms like living wrestlers. If you understand the mechanism, you win the match against forgetting.
Bizarro-Medicine
The permission to sabotage outdated textbooks, question clinical dogma, and let absurdity carry the mechanism home. Blödsinn! rules — but only after peer review.
"No board. No pawns. No 800-page manual. That was a design decision. The complexity is in the cards — and in the mechanism."

What you need

MedKayfab Cards: From any deck (Cardiology, Neurology, Immunology, Nephrology, Pharmacology, etc.). Mix decks freely — the human body doesn't respect specialty boundaries. Each player brings 15–20 cards or share one pool.
Two D6: One Story-Mode Die, one Quest Die. Both stay visible at all times.
1–6 People: And a table. Solo play works too — see Add-Ons.
Optional: A blank card or notebook page for the Mechanism Log.

The Six Clinical Story Modes

"The Mode is a frame, not a cage. If it creates a contradiction with your story — that is the story. Play the contradiction."
1
Lab Report
Dry. Factual. Name the numbers. Sound like a pharma rep reading a package insert — and critique it while you do.
2
Clinical Sabotage
Something goes wrong. A diagnostic error, a drug interaction, a system failure. What does the mechanism do under pressure?
3
Mechanism Flash
Active Recall! Explain the LORE in one sentence without looking. If you do it: Quest Die +1 immediately. If you can't: invent something plausible. The act of trying is the learning.
4
Power-Play
Use the POWER aggressively. The drug hits the receptor. The pathogen evades. The ion channel slams open. Perform the mechanism.
5
Teaching Round
Another player asks one question. Answer in character. No shaming. "I don't know, but here's my best hypothesis" is excellent medicine.
6
Prof. Kayfabian's Blessing
Total anarchy. Play a card directly to the Stage AND move the Quest Die as you see fit. The Professor has arrived.
"Mode 5 is called 'The Teaching Round' — not 'Pimping.' We do not shame people for not knowing things. Here we ask because we're curious. We answer because we're trying."

The Grand Finale

Quest Formats

Standard Quest: Any card can become a Quest. Starting player frames the clinical tension from name and image.
Prepared Quests: Write clinical scenarios on blank cards before playing. "Diagnose the cause of this patient's hyponatraemia before the consultant round."
Mechanism Quests (advanced): The Quest is a mechanism to reconstruct collectively. "Build the complement cascade from C3 to MAC — one card, one step."

Powers & Cards
PERFORM · DON'T CALCULATE

Nothing on a card is a rule. Everything is an invitation. Powers are narrative invitations — not separate mechanics, not damage modifiers. No card overrides this principle.

Anatomy of a Card

Image — Makes a claim without words. Starting point for any story.
LORE — The mechanism or clinical context. Use it or invent from the image.
POWER — What this entity does in the clinical story. Mandatory when introduced. Perform it — don't calculate it.

How to Use a Power

Don't say:
"I block COX-1 and reduce thromboxane by 70%."
Say:
"Every platelet in the bloodstream goes suddenly, permanently quiet. The surgical team will notice this tomorrow. The patient's wife is on warfarin. Nobody checked."
"The Mechanism Log (Optional but Recommended): After each Quest Finale, one player writes down — in one sentence — the core mechanism that emerged from the story. At the end of a session, read the Log aloud. Every mechanism you can name from memory: that's the learning that survived the game."
Learning Add-Ons

Optional layers. Use them when the group wants to study harder, not just play.

Add-On A — Anki Mode

After each Finale, each player selects one card and writes: Front = character name + one clinical clue. Back = core mechanism in one sentence. Add to your Anki deck. Review tomorrow.
"MedKayfab surfaces the card. Anki makes it stick. They're not competing — they're the same memory cycle at different timescales."

Add-On B — Feynman Round

Once per Quest (usually at Quest Die 4), one player picks any card on the Stage and must explain its mechanism to the rest of the group as if they have no medical background. No jargon. One minute max. If the group understands it: Quest Die +1 bonus.
"If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet. Feynman said that. Doc FrizzleBob agrees — reluctantly, because agreeing with physicists feels like admitting defeat."

Add-On C — Spaced Repetition Challenge

Before dealing, shuffle in 3–5 cards from a previous session's Mechanism Log topics, marked with a small dot. When a marked card appears: the player who introduced it last time must explain its mechanism from memory before the Intro Ritual. If they can: +1 Quest Die. If they can't: card still plays, gap is logged.

Add-On D — Peer Teaching Round

After each Quest Finale, one player is randomly chosen (roll D6 — highest = teacher). That player takes one card and explains its mechanism and clinical relevance. Max 3 minutes. The group can ask one question each. "I don't know where my understanding breaks down" is the most valuable teaching there is.

Solo Play — Study Session Mode

Solo setup: Draw 5 cards. Pick one as your Character. Draw a Quest card or write your own. Stage is empty. Quest Die to 1.
Solo Lore-Flash rule: On a roll of 3, cover the LORE text with your hand. Recite it from memory. Then uncover and check. No penalty for being wrong.
"Solo MedKayfab is essentially the Feynman Technique with cards and a die. You pick a concept, you explain it out loud as completely as you can, you find the gaps, you go back. The cards make the gaps visible."
