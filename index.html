Watch: How to Play

A collaborative storytelling card game where every card is a character waiting to be performed. Roll a die for your story mode, introduce a card with a short ritual, tell a story in character, push the Quest Die toward the Finale. No board. No score. No winner. Just a story that didn't exist before you sat down.

Two variants, same bones. Choose your reality:

"This is not a game you play. This is a reality you negotiate, one card at a time, until somebody flinches. Nobody wins. Nobody loses. Everybody leaves slightly changed. That's the plan. Stay fluffy."— Uncle FrizzleBob
What is this?

You tell a shared story using cards, two dice, and whatever's in your head. One sentence at a time — everyone takes turns introducing a card and building on what came before, until the Quest Die hits 6 and the story lands.

Read through Setup, then start. The rest makes sense as you play.

Kayfabe

Wrestling-speak for "the fiction is real." Your story is the reality. Commit to it. If you say there's an invisible elephant in the room, everyone can smell it.

Bizarro

The permission to be strange and illogical. A toaster can be your lawyer. Logic is optional. Surprises are mandatory. Don't explain it — perform it.

"No win condition. No score. Just a story that didn't exist before you sat down — and a sense of whose stories landed. Most people still ask if there's a winner. There isn't. You are the winner. Congratulations."
What you need
Cards: Each player brings 15–20 illustrated cards — tarot, cut-up comics, playing cards with images. Shuffle into one shared deck. Every card can be a Character, Scene, or Quest.
Two D6: One Story-Mode Die, one Quest Die. Both stay visible at all times.
1–6 People: And a table. No board. No pawns. No manual thicker than your wrist.
Setup

Two minutes. Really.

1DealGive 3 secret cards to each player.
2CharactersEach player picks 1 card face-up and runs the Intro Ritual.
3Quest RevealStarter draws a Quest card, runs Intro Ritual. Quest Die to 1.
4StagePlace 3 Scene Cards face-down in the center.
5GoThe fluffiest player starts. You know who that is.
"New to this? Skip the Story-Mode Die for the first round — everyone just runs the Intro Ritual for their Character. Once everyone's in, roll as normal."
The Intro Ritual

Every card that enters the game must be introduced via the Ritual. Think: wrestling entrance. Three beats. Thirty seconds. In character.

Name ItPoint at the card. Announce its name loud. This is who just entered.
Claim ItOne sentence: what does this card want? Read the LORE or invent from the image.
Power ItDon't explain it. Perform it. Show us what this Power does to reality right now.
The Intro Ritual applies to every card, every time — Quest cards, Scene cards, new Characters. Always.
Your Turn

Five steps. Always in this order.

01DrawDraw 1 card. You now have 4 temporarily.
02RollRoll the Story-Mode Die. This is your Mode. Not optional. Commit.
03PlayScene card onto the Stage — or new Character. Always: Intro Ritual.
04TellIn character, in your Mode. Build on the card, weave in the Stage. The Quest must be felt.
05MoveQuest Die: +2, +1, 0, or –1. Your dramaturgical decision.
The Six Story Modes

Roll the Story-Mode Die at the start of your turn. Not optional. Commit to it. The Mode is a frame, not a cage — if it contradicts your story, that is the story.

1TragicLoss is real. Push forward anyway.
2ComicThings go wrong. It's your fault. Play the gap.
3AbsurdThe impossible is normal. Don't explain it.
4HeroicYour Character is right for this moment. Play the peak.
5MysticalSomething's happening beneath the surface.
6ForbiddenCross the line the story's been politely avoiding. Sell it. (Not offensive — necessary.)
"The Mode is a frame, not a cage. I didn't invent the rules of drama — I just put them on dice."
The Stage

The Stage holds three Scene card slots — the shared reality. The cards are the location: "Burning Trashcan" means you're standing next to one.

Empty slot: Fill it with a Scene card.
Stage full (3/3): Overwrite a slot with a yes-and — choose one to discard. Not a yes-but-actually-no.
Character Switch

You can play a Character card into your own slot at any time. Old character leaves or becomes an NPC. Change your voice and posture immediately.

"A good death scene is a gift to the story. Accept gifts."
The Quest Die

A story compass, not a countdown. Not a timer. You move it. You decide when to push or let things breathe.

The Finale

When the Quest Die hits 6, the episode ends. The active player tells the Finale — same 5 steps, but the Quest must resolve.

Memory: Quest card stays as a shared record of this episode.
Clear: Stage cleared. All Scene cards to discard.
New Quest: Next player runs full Quest Reveal ritual.
Reset: Quest Die back to 1. New episode begins.
"The Finale doesn't have to be good. It has to be true — true to what everyone built. A messy ending that was earned beats a neat ending that was imported."
Optional Rules
Quest Fail: Die below 1 → Quest fails. No Finale, no memory. The collapse is the story.
Staged Clearing: Roll after Finale. 1–2: remove 1 Scene. 3–4: remove 2. 5–6: all three. Survivors carry history forward.
Social Calls

After a story — before the next player starts. Quest Die move stands unless Blödsinn! lands.

KayfaBINGO!That landed. Pure praise. Storyteller draws an extra card.
KayfaBOGGLE?I'm lost. One concrete question. Active player answers in 1–2 sentences, in character.
KayfaBONGO!Power resolved like a boardgame stat — call it. Restate as story, not transaction.
BLÖDSINN!Rule broken: out of character, Scene forgotten, Mode ignored. Majority decides. If stands: Quest Die does not move.
Table Culture
Kayfab-Finger Rule: Point at every card as you mention it.
Silence Is Allowed: 10 seconds to think. Don't rush the Bizarro.
Overacting Is Encouraged: If you're not embarrassed, you're not playing hard enough.
X-Card: Tap the table to stop and reset a scene. No questions asked.
Laughter Breaks Nothing: If everyone's laughing, you're playing it right.
Solo Play — The Lonely Rabbit Mode

Play all Characters yourself. Switch seats or hats. After each story bit, write one sentence about what shifted — this replaces the Social Calls.

"I'm my own best audience anyway."
Powers & Cards

Nothing on a card is a rule. Everything on a card is an invitation to perform.

Anatomy of a Card
Image — Makes a claim without words. Starting point.
Quote — The card's voice. Anchor for your narration.
Lore — Context or backstory. The "Why." Use it or ignore it.
Power — What this card does in the story. Mandatory when introduced.
How to Use a Power

Powers are narrative — not mechanics, not modifiers, not damage. Read the Power aloud in the Ritual, then show us what it looks like when it comes alive.

Don't say
"I deal 3 damage to you."
Say instead
"Your empire glitches. The generals forget what war is. Seven kinds of collapse happen at once."
"Powers are the only way to legally cheat. Use them. If a Power is boring, make it weird."
Stacking Powers

Resolve in the order they were played. If they contradict each other, that contradiction is the scene. Play it.

"Forget rote memorization and mindless lists. We are here to wrestle with physiology, outwit the pathogen, and diagnose the system — the biological one and the broken one. One sentence at a time, until the case resolves, the patient survives, or the mechanism finally makes sense. Stay fluffy — and always read the primary literature."— Doc FrizzleBob, Professor of Anarcho-Medicine & Reluctant Attending
What is this?

You each play a Character — a clinician, a drug, an immune cell, a rogue signalling molecule, or a personified disease. Together you build a shared Clinical Stage and push a Medical Quest toward its resolution.

No win condition. No score. Just a clinical story that didn't exist before you sat down — and a mechanism you won't forget.

MedKayfab

"The pathophysiology is real." We treat biological mechanisms like living wrestlers. If you understand the mechanism, you win the match against forgetting.

Bizarro-Medicine

The permission to sabotage outdated textbooks, question clinical dogma, and let absurdity carry the mechanism home — but only after peer review.

What you need
MedKayfab Cards: From any deck — Cardiology, Neurology, Immunology, Pharmacology. Mix freely. The human body doesn't respect specialty boundaries.
Two D6: One Story-Mode Die, one Quest Die. Both visible at all times.
Optional: A blank card or notebook for the Mechanism Log.
Setup

2 minutes. Scrub in.

1DealGive 3 cards to each player. Keep them secret.
2CharactersEveryone picks 1 card face-up. Run the Intro Ritual — in character, out loud.
3Quest RevealStarting player draws Quest card, runs Intro Ritual, frames clinical tension. Quest Die to 1.
4GoStage empty. Starting player tells the first story.
"You will feel slightly ridiculous for the first few minutes. That's fine. The ridiculous is where the mechanism is memorable."
The Intro Ritual

Every card that enters play gets the same short ritual. Think: the moment the diagnosis is announced. Three beats. Thirty seconds. In character.

Name ItAnnounce the card's name and the clinical concept. Loud.
Claim ItOne sentence: what is its mechanism? Read the LORE or invent from the image.
Power ItDon't explain it. Perform it. Show us what this Power does to the patient right now.
"The Intro isn't a flashcard. It's a promise. The mechanism becomes real the moment you commit to it."
The Intro Ritual applies to every card, every time. Always.
Your Turn

Five steps. Always in this order.

01DrawDraw 1 card (temporarily 4 hand cards).
02RollRoll the Story-Mode Die. This is your Clinical Mode. Commit to it.
03PlayScene card onto Stage — or new Character. Always: Intro Ritual.
04TellIn character, in your Mode. Build on the card, weave in the Stage. The Quest must be felt. Power performed → discard.
05MoveQuest Die: +2, +1, 0, or –1. Never below 1.
The Six Clinical Modes

Roll the Story-Mode Die. Not optional. Commit to it.

1Lab ReportDry. Factual. Name the numbers. Sound like a pharma rep — and critique it while you do.
2Clin. SabotageSomething goes wrong. A diagnostic error, a drug interaction. What does the mechanism do under pressure?
3Mech. FlashActive Recall. Explain the LORE in one sentence without looking. If you do it: Quest Die +1 immediately.
4Power-PlayUse the Power aggressively. The drug hits the receptor. The pathogen evades. Perform the mechanism.
5Teaching RoundAnother player asks one question. Answer in character. No shaming. "Here's my best hypothesis" is excellent medicine.
6Prof. BlessingTotal anarchy. Play a card directly to the Stage and move the Quest Die as you see fit.
"Mode 5 is 'The Teaching Round' — not 'Pimping.' We ask because we're curious. We answer because we're trying."

Empty slot: Fill it with a Scene card.

Quest Formats
Standard Quest: Any card. Starting player frames the clinical tension.
Prepared Quests: Write clinical scenarios on blank cards. "Diagnose the cause of this patient's hyponatraemia before the consultant round."
Mechanism Quests: A mechanism to reconstruct collectively. "Build the complement cascade from C3 to MAC — one card, one step."
The Grand Finale

When the Quest Die hits 6, the active player tells the Finale. The Quest must resolve. It must land.

Memory: Quest card stays on table. Write the mechanism on it if running the Mechanism Log.
Tabula Rasa: Stage clears. All Scene cards to discard.
New Quest: Next player runs Quest Reveal. Quest Die to 1.
"A messy ending that was earned beats a clean answer that was imported from the index."

Solo Play — Study Session Mode

Draw 5 cards. Pick one as Character. Draw or write a Quest. Stage empty. Quest Die to 1. Play all Characters yourself. After each story: write one sentence in the Mechanism Log.

"Solo MedKayfab is essentially the Feynman Technique with cards and a die. The cards make the gaps visible."
Powers & Cards

Nothing on a card is a rule. Powers are narrative invitations — not mechanics, not modifiers.

Anatomy of a Card
Image — Makes a claim without words.
LORE — The mechanism or clinical context. Use it or invent from the image.
Power — What this entity does in the clinical story. Mandatory when introduced. Perform it — don't calculate it.
How to Use a Power
Don't say
"I block COX-1 and reduce thromboxane by 70%."
Say instead
"Every platelet in the bloodstream goes suddenly, permanently quiet. The surgical team will notice this tomorrow. The patient's wife is on warfarin. Nobody checked."
The Mechanism Log

After each Quest Finale, one player writes — in one sentence — the core mechanism that emerged. At the end of a session, read the Log aloud. Every mechanism you can name from memory: that's the learning that survived.

"The Mechanism Log is not homework. It's evidence that the session produced something real."
Learning Add-Ons

Optional layers for when the group wants to study harder, not just play.

Add-On A — Anki Mode
After each Finale: Front = character name + clinical clue. Back = core mechanism in one sentence. Add to Anki. Review tomorrow.
Add-On B — Feynman Round
Once per Quest, one player explains a Stage card's mechanism — no jargon, one minute max. Group understands it: Quest Die +1 immediately.
Add-On C — Spaced Repetition
Shuffle in 3–5 marked cards (small dot) from previous sessions. When one appears: explain from memory before the Intro Ritual. Success: +1 Quest Die.
Add-On D — Peer Teaching Round
After each Finale, roll D6 — highest = teacher. 3 minutes, one card, mechanism + clinical relevance. Group asks one question each.
"The best way to learn something is to teach it. The best way to teach it is to not be afraid of the gap."
