What's the game?

A collaborative storytelling card game where every card is a character waiting to be performed. Roll a die for your story mode, introduce a card with a theatrical ritual, tell a short story in character, push the Quest Die toward the Finale. No board. No score. No winner. Just a story that didn't exist before you sat down — and a deep suspicion that reality was negotiable all along.

Two variants, same bones. Choose your reality:

KAYFABIZARRO FREESTYLE

The Social Story-Wrestling Card Game · 1–6 Players · 20–60 Min · CC BY-NC-SA 4.0
"This is not a game you play. This is a reality you negotiate, one card at a time, until somebody flinches. Nobody wins. Nobody loses. Everybody leaves slightly changed. That's the plan. Stay fluffy."— Uncle FrizzleBob (Professional Reality-Wrestler & Epistemiologic Outlaw)
What is this?

You tell a shared story using cards, two dice, and whatever's in your head. One sentence at a time — everyone takes turns introducing a card and building on what came before, until the Quest Die hits 6 and the story lands.

Read through Setup, then start. The rest makes sense as you play.

Kayfabe

Wrestling-speak for "the fiction is real." Your story is the reality. Commit to it — no winking at the camera. If you say there's an invisible elephant in the room, everyone can smell it.

Bizarro

The permission to be strange and illogical. A toaster can be your lawyer. Logic is optional. Surprises are mandatory. Don't explain it — perform it.

"No win condition. No score. Just a story that didn't exist before you sat down — and a sense of whose stories landed. Most people still ask if there's a winner. There isn't. You are the winner. Congratulations."
What you need
Cards: Each player brings 15–20 illustrated cards — tarot, cut-up comics, playing cards with images. Shuffle into one shared face-down deck. Every card can be a Character, Scene, or Quest.
Two D6: One Story-Mode Die, one Quest Die. Both stay visible at all times.
1–6 People: And a table. No board. No pawns. No manual thicker than your wrist.
Setup

Two minutes. Really.

1
Deal

Give 3 secret cards to each player.

2
Characters

Each player picks 1 card face-up and runs the Intro Ritual.

3
Quest Reveal

Starter draws a Quest card, runs Intro Ritual. Quest Die to 1.

4
Stage

Place 3 Scene Cards face-down in the center.

5
Go

The fluffiest player starts. You know who that is.

"New to this? Skip the Story-Mode Die for the first round — everyone just runs the Intro Ritual for their Character. Once everyone's in, roll as normal. I'm generous like that."
The Intro Ritual

Every card that enters the game must be introduced via the Ritual. Think: wrestling entrance. The spotlight hits. You walk out and you are whoever the card says you are. Three beats. Thirty seconds. In character.

Name It

Point at the card. Announce its name loud. This is who just entered.

Claim It

One sentence: what does this card want? Read the LORE or invent from the image.

Power It

Don't explain it. Perform it. Show us what this Power does to reality right now.

The Intro Ritual applies to every card, every time — Quest cards, Scene cards, new Characters. Always. The story starts the moment you commit.
Your Turn

Five steps. Always in this order. Simple as a wrestling card — perform it with style.

01
Draw

Draw 1 card. You now have 4 temporarily.

02
Roll

Roll the Story-Mode Die. This is your Mode. Not optional. Commit.

03
Play

Scene card onto the Stage — or new Character. Always: Intro Ritual.

04
Tell

In character, in your Mode. Build on the card, weave in the Stage. Quest must be felt.

05
Move

Quest Die: +2, +1, 0, or –1. Your dramaturgical decision.

The Six Story Modes

At the start of your turn, roll the Story-Mode Die. This is your Mode. Not optional. Commit to it. The Mode is a frame, not a cage — if it creates a contradiction with your story, that is the story. Play the contradiction.

1
Tragic
Loss is real. Push forward anyway.
2
Comic
Things go wrong. It's your fault. Play the gap.
3
Absurd
The impossible is normal. Don't explain it.
4
Heroic
Your Character is right for this moment. Play the peak.
5
Mystical
Something's happening beneath the surface.
6
Forbidden
Cross the line the story's been politely avoiding. Sell it. (Doesn't mean offensive — means necessary.)
"The Mode is a frame, not a cage. I didn't invent the rules of drama — I just put them on dice."
The Stage

The Stage holds three Scene card slots — the shared reality. The cards are the location. "Burning Trashcan" means you're standing next to a burning trashcan.

Empty slot: Fill it with a Scene card.
Stage full (3/3): Overwrite a slot with a yes-and — choose one to discard. Make it dramatic. Not a yes-but-actually-no.
Character Switch

You can play a Character card into your own slot at any time. Your old character either leaves the story or becomes an NPC controlled by the table. Change your voice and posture immediately.

"A good death scene is a gift to the story. Accept gifts."
The Quest Die

The Quest Die is a story compass, not a countdown. Not a timer. You move it. You decide when to push hard or let things breathe.

Setup
3–4Akt II
Escalation
5Ordeal
Crisis
6FINALE
It ends.
+2 — Hard push. Accelerating toward chaos. Use sparingly.
+1 — Normal progression. The story moves forward.
0 — Hold. Linger. Build character. No rush.
–1 — Step back. When a twist reframes everything, or the next player needs room to build.
Quest Die never goes below 1. At 6: Finale triggered on the next turn.
The Finale

When the Quest Die hits 6, the episode ends. The active player tells the Finale on their next turn — same 5 steps, but the Quest must resolve.

Memory: Quest card stays on the table as a shared record of this episode.
Clear: Stage cleared. All Scene cards to discard.
New Quest: Next player draws a card and runs the full Quest Reveal ritual.
Reset: Quest Die back to 1. New episode begins.
"The Finale doesn't have to be good. It has to be true — true to what everyone built. A messy ending that was earned beats a neat ending that was imported from somewhere else."
Optional Rules
Quest Fail: Die below 1 → Quest fails without resolution. No Finale, no memory. The collapse is the story.
Staged Clearing: Roll after Finale. 1–2: remove 1 Scene. 3–4: remove 2. 5–6: all three. Survivors carry their history into the next episode.
Social Calls

After a story — before the next player starts — anyone can call one of these. Your Quest Die move stands unless Blödsinn! lands.

KayfaBINGO!
That landed.
Pure praise. Spontaneous, no explanation needed. Storyteller draws an extra card. Shout it. Mean it.
KayfaBOGGLE?
I'm lost.
One concrete question — not a judgment, a gap. Active player answers in 1–2 sentences, in character. Play continues.
KayfaBONGO!
Power as mechanic.
Someone resolved a Power like a boardgame ("I deal 3 damage") — call it. They restate as story, not transaction.
BLÖDSINN!
Broke Kayfabe.
Rule broken: out of character, Scene forgotten, Mode ignored. Majority decides. If stands: Quest Die does not move.
"King Kayfabian (Optional): Final call on disputes. Passes left after one story. No permanent power. Most tables won't need this. Most tables."
Table Culture — Non-Negotiable
Kayfab-Finger Rule: Point at every card as you mention it. Keeps everyone focused and makes you look slightly unhinged. That's the point.
Silence Is Allowed: 10 seconds to think. Don't rush the Bizarro.
Overacting Is Encouraged: If you're not embarrassed, you're not playing hard enough.
X-Card: Tap the table to stop and reset a scene. No questions asked. The story serves the people.
Laughter Breaks Nothing: If everyone's laughing, you're playing it right.
Solo Play — The Lonely Rabbit Mode

Play all Characters yourself. Switch seats or hats to track who is who. After each story bit, write one sentence about what shifted — this replaces the Social Calls.

"I'm my own best audience anyway."
Powers & Cards

Nothing on a card is a rule. Everything on a card is an invitation to perform.

Anatomy of a Card
Image — Makes a claim without words. Starting point for any story.
Quote — Gives the card a voice. Anchor for your narration.
Lore — Context or backstory. Use it or ignore it. The "Why."
Power — What this card does in the story. Mandatory when introduced. Takes priority over everything else.
How to Use a Power

Powers are narrative — not mechanics, not modifiers, not damage counters. Read the Power aloud in the Intro Ritual, then show us what it looks like when it comes alive.

Don't say

"I deal 3 damage to you."

Say instead

"Your empire glitches. The generals forget what war is. Seven kinds of collapse happen at once."

"Powers are the only way to legally cheat. Use them. If a Power is boring, make it weird. That's not a rule — that's just good advice."
Stacking Powers

If multiple Powers are active, resolve them in the order they were played. If they contradict each other, that contradiction is the scene. Play it.

MEDKAYFAB — Mnemonic Medicine Mayhem

The Clinical Story Wrestling Card Game · Solo or 1–6 Students/Outlaws · 20–60 Min · CC BY-NC-SA 4.0
"Forget rote memorization and mindless lists. We are here to wrestle with physiology, outwit the pathogen, and diagnose the system — the biological one and the broken one. One sentence at a time, until the case resolves, the patient survives, or the mechanism finally makes sense. Stay fluffy — and always read the primary literature."

— Doc FrizzleBob, Professor of Anarcho-Medicine, Epistemic Outlaw & Reluctant Attending
What is this?

You each play a Character — a clinician, a drug, an immune cell, a rogue signalling molecule, or a personified disease. Together you build a shared Clinical Stage and push a Medical Quest toward its resolution.

The Quest Die tracks how close the case is to resolution — but it's not a timer. You move it. When it reaches 6, someone tells the Finale: the diagnosis is made, the mechanism is revealed, the patient survives (or doesn't).

No win condition. No score. Just a clinical story that didn't exist before you sat down — and a mechanism you won't forget.

MedKayfab

"The pathophysiology is real." We treat biological mechanisms like living wrestlers. If you understand the mechanism, you win the match against forgetting.

Bizarro-Medicine

The permission to sabotage outdated textbooks, question clinical dogma, and let absurdity carry the mechanism home. Where evidence-based medicine drowns in bureaucracy, we let Blödsinn! rule — but only after we've peer reviewed why the bureaucracy exists.

What you need
MedKayfab Cards: From any deck — Cardiology, Neurology, Immunology, Nephrology, Pharmacology. Mix freely. The human body doesn't respect specialty boundaries.
Two D6: One Story-Mode Die, one Quest Die. Both visible at all times.
Optional: A blank card or notebook page for the Mechanism Log.

The Six Clinical Modes

At the start of your turn, roll the Story-Mode Die. Not optional. Commit to it. The Mode is a frame, not a cage — if it creates a contradiction with your story, that is the story.

1
Lab Report
Dry. Factual. Name the numbers. Sound like a pharma rep reading a package insert — and critique it while you do.
2
Clinical Sabotage
Something goes wrong. A diagnostic error, a drug interaction, a system failure. What does the mechanism do under pressure?
3
Mechanism Flash
Active Recall! Explain the LORE in one sentence without looking. If you do it: Quest Die +1 immediately. Invent if you can't — the act of trying is the learning.
4
Power-Play
Use the POWER aggressively. The drug hits the receptor. The pathogen evades. The ion channel slams open. Perform the mechanism.
5
Teaching Round
Another player asks one question. Answer in character. No shaming. "I don't know, but here's my best hypothesis" is excellent medicine.
6
Prof. Kayfabian's Blessing
Total anarchy. Play a card directly to the Stage and move the Quest Die as you see fit. The Professor has arrived.
"Mode 5 is called 'The Teaching Round' — not 'Pimping.' We do not shame people for not knowing things. Here we ask because we're curious. We answer because we're trying."

Quest Formats
Standard Quest: Any card can become a Quest. Starting player frames the clinical tension from the card's name and image.
Prepared Quests: Write clinical scenarios on blank cards. "Diagnose the cause of this patient's hyponatraemia before the consultant round."
Mechanism Quests (advanced): The Quest is a mechanism to reconstruct collectively. "Build the complement cascade from C3 to MAC — one card, one step."
The Grand Finale

When the Quest Die hits 6, the active player tells the Finale on their next turn. Normal story. The Quest must resolve. It must land.

Memory: Quest card stays on table. Write the mechanism on it if running the Mechanism Log.
Tabula Rasa: Stage clears. All Scene cards to discard.
New Quest: Next player runs Quest Reveal. Quest Die to 1.
"The Finale doesn't have to be a textbook answer. It has to be true to what everyone built. A messy ending that was earned beats a clean answer that was imported from the index."

Solo Play — Study Session Mode

Draw 5 cards. Pick one as Character. Draw or write a Quest. Stage empty. Quest Die to 1. Play all Characters yourself. After each story: write one sentence in the Mechanism Log.

Solo Lore-Flash rule: On a roll of 3, cover the LORE text. Recite from memory. Uncover and check. No penalty for being wrong.

"Solo MedKayfab is essentially the Feynman Technique with cards and a die. The cards make the gaps visible."
Powers & Cards

Nothing on a card is a rule. Everything is an invitation. Powers are narrative invitations — not separate mechanics, not damage modifiers. No card overrides this principle.

Anatomy of a Card
Image — Makes a claim without words. Starting point for any story.
LORE — The mechanism or clinical context. Use it or invent from the image.
POWER — What this entity does in the clinical story. Mandatory when introduced. Perform it — don't calculate it.
How to Use a Power
Don't say

"I block COX-1 and reduce thromboxane by 70%."

Say instead

"Every platelet in the bloodstream goes suddenly, permanently quiet. The surgical team will notice this tomorrow. The patient's wife is on warfarin. Nobody checked."

The Mechanism Log

After each Quest Finale, one player writes — in one sentence — the core mechanism that emerged from the story. At the end of a session, read the Log aloud. Every mechanism you can name from memory: that's the learning that survived the game.

"The Mechanism Log is not homework. It's evidence that the session produced something real."
Learning Add-Ons

Optional layers. Use them when the group wants to study harder, not just play.

Add-On A — Anki Mode
After each Finale, each player selects one card and writes: Front = character name + clinical clue. Back = core mechanism in one sentence. Add to Anki. Review tomorrow.
"MedKayfab surfaces the card. Anki makes it stick. Same memory cycle, different timescales."
Add-On B — Feynman Round
Once per Quest, one player explains a Stage card's mechanism to the group — no jargon, no acronyms, one minute max. Group understands it: Quest Die +1 bonus immediately.
"If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet. Feynman said that. Doc FrizzleBob agrees — reluctantly, because agreeing with physicists feels like admitting defeat."
Add-On C — Spaced Repetition Challenge
Before dealing, shuffle in 3–5 marked cards (small dot on back) from previous sessions. When a marked card appears: the player who introduced it last must explain its mechanism from memory before the Intro Ritual. Success: +1 Quest Die. Failure: card still plays, gap logged.
Add-On D — Peer Teaching Round
After each Finale, roll D6 — highest = teacher. 3 minutes, one card, mechanism + clinical relevance. Group asks one question each. "Here's where my understanding breaks down" is the most valuable teaching there is.
