## What is this?

You tell a shared story using cards, two dice, and whatever's in your head. One sentence at a time. Everyone takes turns introducing a card and building on what came before, until the Quest Die hits 6 and the story lands.

Read this section and Setup, then start. The rest makes sense as you play.

### Kayfabe

Wrestling-speak for "the fiction is real." Your story is the reality. Commit to it — no winking at the camera. If you say there's an invisible elephant in the room, everyone can smell it.

### Bizarro

The permission to be strange and illogical. A toaster can be your lawyer. Logic is optional. Surprises are mandatory. Don't explain it — perform it.

> *"No win condition. No score. Just a story that didn't exist before you sat down — and a sense of whose stories landed. That's the whole game. I've been telling people this for years. Most of them still ask if there's a winner. There isn't. You are the winner. Congratulations."*

### What you need

- **Cards:** Each player brings 15–20 illustrated cards — tarot, cut-up comics, playing cards with images. Shuffle everything into one shared face-down deck. Every card can be a Character, Scene, or Quest.
- **Two D6:** One Story-Mode Die, one Quest Die. Both stay visible at all times.
- **1–6 People:** And a table. Optional: a sheet of paper for doodling as the story grows. No board. No pawns. No manual thicker than your wrist.

## Setup

*2 minutes. Really.*

1. **Deal** — Give 3 secret cards to each player. These are your hand.
2. **Characters** — Each player picks 1 card face-up and runs the Intro Ritual. This is your Character — your "skin."
3. **Quest Reveal** — Starter draws a Quest card, runs Intro Ritual. Place the Quest Die next to it, showing 1.
4. **The Stage** — Place 3 Scene Cards face-down in the center. These are the empty Slots.
5. **Go** — The fluffiest player starts. You know who that is.

> *"New to this? Skip the Story-Mode Die for the first round — everyone just runs the Intro Ritual for their Character. Once everyone's in, roll as normal. I'm generous like that."*

### The Intro Ritual — Name It / Claim It / Power It

Every card that enters the game must be introduced via the Ritual. Think: wrestling entrance. The spotlight hits. You walk out and you ARE whoever the card says you are. Three beats. Thirty seconds. In character.

| Step | What you do |
|---|---|
| **Name It** | Point at the card. Announce its name loud. This is who or what just entered. |
| **Claim It** | One sentence: what does this card want? Read it off the card or invent it from the image. |
| **Power It** | Don't explain it. Perform it. Show us what this Power does to reality right now. |

> **NOTE:** The Intro Ritual applies to every card, every time — Quest cards, Scene cards, new Characters. Always. The story starts the moment you commit.

## Your Turn

Five steps. Always in this order. Simple as a wrestling card — perform it with style.

| Step | Name | What you do |
|---|---|---|
| 01 | **Draw** | Draw 1 card from the shared deck. You now have 4 hand cards temporarily. |
| 02 | **Roll** | Roll the Story-Mode Die. This is your Mode for this turn. Not optional. Commit to it. |
| 03 | **Play** | Choose one card. Play it as a Scene card onto the Stage — or as your new Character. Either way: run the Intro Ritual. |
| 04 | **Tell** | Tell a story in character, in your Mode. Build on the card you played, weave in the other Scene cards. The Quest must be felt. |
| 05 | **Move** | Move the Quest Die: +2, +1, 0, or –1. Your call. Your dramaturgical decision. |

### The Six Story Modes

> *"The Mode is a frame, not a cage. If it creates a contradiction with your story, that IS the story. Play the contradiction. I didn't invent the rules of drama — I just put them on dice."*

| Roll | Mode | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Tragic** | Loss is real. Push forward anyway. |
| 2 | **Comic** | Things go wrong. It's your fault. Play the gap. |
| 3 | **Absurd** | The impossible is normal. Don't explain it. |
| 4 | **Heroic** | Your Character is right for this moment. Play the peak. |
| 5 | **Mystical** | Something's happening beneath the surface. |
| 6 | **Forbidden** | Cross the line the story's been politely avoiding. Sell it. (Doesn't mean offensive — means necessary.) |

### The Stage — 3 Scene Slots

- The Stage holds three Scene cards — the shared reality. The cards ARE the location. "Burning Trashcan" means you're standing next to a burning trashcan.
- Stage full (3/3)? Overwrite a slot with a yes-and. Not a yes-but-actually-no. Choose one to discard. Make it dramatic.

### Character Switch

You can play a Character card into your own slot at any time. Your old character either leaves the story or becomes an NPC controlled by the table. Change your voice and posture immediately.

> *"A good death scene is a gift to the story. Accept gifts."*

## The Quest Die

The Quest Die is a story compass, not a countdown. Not a timer. You move it. You decide when to push hard or let things breathe.

| Die Value | Act | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | Akt I | Setup — Establish the world, introduce Characters. |
| 3–4 | Akt II | Escalation — Conflict. Things escalate. Something breaks. |
| 5 | Ordeal | Crisis — Darkest moment. Something must give. |
| 6 | **FINALE** | It ends. Now. |

### Moving the Die

- **+2** — Hard push. Accelerating toward chaos. Use sparingly.
- **+1** — Normal progression. The story moves forward.
- **0** — Hold. Linger. Build character. No rush.
- **–1** — Step back. When a twist reframes everything, or the next player needs room to build.

> **NOTE:** Quest Die never goes below 1. At 6: Finale is triggered next turn.

### The Finale — Quest Collapse

When the Quest Die hits 6, the episode ends. The active player tells the Finale — same 5 steps, but the Quest must resolve.

1. **Memory** — Quest card stays on the table as a shared record of this episode.
2. **Clear** — Stage is cleared. All Scene cards to discard.
3. **New Quest** — Next player draws a card and runs the full Quest Reveal ritual.
4. **Reset** — Quest Die back to 1. New episode begins.

> *"The Finale doesn't have to be good. It has to be true — true to what everyone built together. A messy ending that was earned beats a neat ending that was imported from somewhere else."*

### Optional Rules

- **Quest Fail** — If the Quest Die is moved below 1, the Quest fails without resolution. No Finale, no memory. The collapse is the story. Start again immediately.
- **Staged Clearing** — Roll Story-Mode Die after the Finale. 1–2: remove 1 Scene. 3–4: remove 2. 5–6: all three. Survivors carry their history into the new episode.

## Social Calls

After a story — before the next player starts — anyone can call one of these. Your Quest Die move stands unless Blödsinn! lands.

| Call | Effect | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **KayfaBINGO!** | That landed. | Spontaneous, no explanation needed. Pure praise. The storyteller draws an extra card. Shout it. Mean it. |
| **KayfaBOGGLE?** | I'm lost. | One concrete question — not a judgment, a gap. Active player answers in 1–2 sentences, in character. Play continues. |
| **KayfaBONGO!** | Power as mechanic. | Someone resolved a Power like a boardgame ("I deal 3 damage") — call it. They restate as story, not transaction. |
| **BLÖDSINN!** | Broke Kayfabe. | A rule was broken: out of character, Scene card forgotten, Mode ignored. Majority decides. If it stands: Quest Die does not move. |

> *"King Kayfabian (Optional): If calls are causing arguments, one player takes the King Kayfabian role — final call on any dispute. Passes left after one story. No permanent power. Most tables won't need this. Most tables."*

### Table Culture — Non-Negotiable

- **Kayfab-Finger Rule** — Point at every card as you mention it. Keeps everyone focused and makes you look slightly unhinged. That's the point.
- **Silence Is Allowed** — You have 10 seconds to think. Silence is a valid move. Don't rush the Bizarro.
- **Overacting Is Encouraged** — Big gestures, strange voices, exaggerated drama. If you're not embarrassed, you're not playing hard enough.
- **X-Card** — Tap the table to stop and reset a scene. No questions asked. The story serves the people, not the other way around.
- **Laughter Breaks Nothing** — If everyone's laughing, you're playing it right.

### Solo Play — The Lonely Rabbit Mode

Play all Characters yourself. Switch seats or hats to track who is who. After each story bit, write one sentence about what shifted — this replaces the Social Calls.

> *"I'm my own best audience anyway."*

## Powers & Cards

*Nothing on a card is a rule. Everything on a card is an invitation to perform.*

### The Anatomy of a Card

- **Image** — Makes a claim without words. Starting point for any story.
- **Quote** — Gives the card a voice. Anchor for your narration.
- **Lore** — Context or backstory. Use it or ignore it. This is the "Why."
- **Power** — What this card does in the story. Mandatory when introduced. Takes priority over everything else.

### How to Use a Power

Powers are narrative — not mechanics, not modifiers, not damage counters. When you introduce a card, read the Power aloud in the Intro Ritual. Then show us what it looks like when that Power comes alive.

| Don't say | Say instead |
|---|---|
| "I deal 3 damage to you." | "Your empire glitches. The generals forget what war is. Seven kinds of collapse happen at once." |

> *"If you wove the Power into your story, the card goes to discard. If you just described it but didn't really perform it, it stays on the Stage until overwritten. I trust you to know the difference. Probably."*

### Stacking Powers

If multiple Powers are active, resolve them in the order they were played. If they contradict each other, that contradiction is the scene. Play it.

> **NOTE:** Powers are the only way to legally cheat. Use them. If a Power is boring, make it weird. That's not a rule — that's just good advice.
