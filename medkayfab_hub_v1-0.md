# MedKayfab: Mnemonic Medicine Mayhem — Hub Rules v1.0

> Doc FrizzleBob's Medical Kard Kult · v1.0  
> The Clinical Story Wrestling Card Game · Solo or 1–6 Students/Outlaws · 20–60 Min · CC BY-NC-SA 4.0

*"Forget rote memorization and mindless lists. We are here to wrestle with physiology, outwit the pathogen, and diagnose the system — the biological one and the broken one. One sentence at a time, until the case resolves, the patient survives, or the mechanism finally makes sense."*  
— Doc FrizzleBob (Professor of Anarcho-Medicine, Epistemic Outlaw & Reluctant Attending)

---

## What is this?

You each play a Character — a clinician, a drug, an immune cell, a rogue signalling molecule, or a personified disease. Together you build a shared Clinical Stage and push a Medical Quest toward its resolution.

On your turn: roll to find out what kind of story you're telling, introduce a card with a short ritual, and tell a part of the story. The Quest Die tracks how close the case is to resolution — but it's not a timer. You move it.

When the Quest Die reaches 6, someone tells the Finale — the diagnosis is made, the mechanism is revealed, the patient survives (or doesn't). Quest resolved. New case.

No win condition. No score. Just a clinical story that didn't exist before you sat down — and a mechanism you won't forget.

### MedKayfab

"The pathophysiology is real." We treat biological mechanisms like living wrestlers. If you understand the mechanism, you win the match against forgetting.

### Bizarro-Medicine

The permission to sabotage outdated textbooks, question clinical dogma, and let absurdity carry the mechanism home. Where evidence-based medicine drowns in bureaucracy, we let Blödsinn! rule — but only after we've peer reviewed why the bureaucracy exists.

> *"No board. No pawns. No 800-page manual. That was a design decision. The complexity is in the cards — and in the mechanism."*

### What you need

- **MedKayfab Cards:** From any deck (Cardiology, Neurology, Immunology, Nephrology, Pharmacology, etc.). Mix decks freely — the human body doesn't respect specialty boundaries anyway. Each player brings 15–20 cards or share one shuffled pool.
- **Two D6:** One Story-Mode Die, one Quest Die. Both stay visible at all times.
- **1–6 People:** And a table. Solo play: see Solo Play section below.
- **Optional:** A blank card or notebook page for the Mechanism Log.

## Setup

*2 minutes. You will feel slightly ridiculous for the first few minutes. That's fine. The ridiculous is where the mechanism is memorable.*

1. **Deal** — Give 3 cards to each player. Keep them secret.
2. **Characters** — Everyone picks 1 card, places it face-up. Run the Intro Ritual — in character, out loud.
3. **Quest Reveal** — Starting player draws a Quest card, places it next to the Stage, runs the Intro Ritual, and frames the clinical tension. Quest Die to 1.
4. **Go** — Stage is empty. Starting player tells the first story.

> **New to this?** Skip the Story-Mode Die for the first round of introductions — everyone just runs the Intro Ritual for their Character. Once everyone's in, roll as normal.

### The Intro Ritual — Perform the Mechanism

Every card that enters play gets the same short ritual. Think: the moment the diagnosis is announced. The spotlight hits. You name the entity and tell us what it does. Three beats. Thirty seconds. In character.

| Step | What you do |
|---|---|
| **Name It** | Announce the card's character name AND the actual clinical concept. Loud. This is who just entered the case. |
| **Claim It** | One sentence: what does this entity want? What is its mechanism? Read the LORE or invent from the image. |
| **Power It** | Don't explain it. Perform it. Show us what this Power does to the patient right now. |

> *"The Intro isn't a flashcard. It's a promise. Say it like you mean it — the mechanism becomes real the moment you commit to it."*

## Your Turn

Five steps. Always in this order.

| Step | Name | What you do |
|---|---|---|
| 01 | **Draw** | Draw 1 card from the shared deck. You now have 4 hand cards temporarily. |
| 02 | **Roll** | Roll the Story-Mode Die. This is your Mode for this turn. Not optional. Commit to it. |
| 03 | **Play** | Choose one card. Play it as a Scene card onto the Stage — or as your new Character (Character Switch). Either way: run the Intro Ritual. |
| 04 | **Tell** | Tell a story in character, in your Mode. Build on the card you played, weave in the other Scene cards. The Quest must be felt. Power performed → card goes to discard. |
| 05 | **Move** | Quest Die: +2, +1, 0, or –1. Your dramaturgical decision. Quest Die never goes below 1. At 6: Finale next turn. |

> *"Why would you ever choose –1? When a complication reframes everything. When the next player needs room to build the diagnosis. The Quest Die is yours to conduct — not just push forward."*

### The Six Clinical Story Modes

| Roll | Mode | Play it like… |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Lab Report** | Dry. Factual. Use the STAT block. Name the numbers. Sound like a pharma rep reading a package insert — and critique it while you do. |
| 2 | **Clinical Sabotage** | Something goes wrong. A diagnostic error, a drug interaction, a system failure. How does your card react? What does the mechanism do under pressure? |
| 3 | **Mechanism Flash** | Active Recall! Explain the mechanism of your card (LORE) in one sentence — without looking at it. If you do it without reading: Quest Die +1 immediately. If you can't: invent something plausible and explain it anyway. The act of trying is the learning. |
| 4 | **Power-Play** | Use the POWER of your card aggressively. The drug hits the receptor. The pathogen evades the immune response. The ion channel slams open. Describe the "special attack" of the molecule. |
| 5 | **Teaching Round** | Another player — the Attending — asks you one question about your card. Answer it in character. No shaming. No pressure. Making something up is allowed. Saying "I don't know, but here's my best hypothesis" is excellent medicine. |
| 6 | **Prof. Kayfabian's Blessing** | Total anarchy. Play a card from your hand directly to the Stage AND move the Quest Die as you see fit. The Professor has arrived. The clinical case has just become more interesting. |

> *"Mode 5 is called 'The Teaching Round' — not 'Pimping.' We do not shame people for not knowing things. Real pimping is a feature of a broken system, not a teaching method. Here we ask because we're curious. We answer because we're trying."*

### The Clinical Stage — 3 Scene Slots

- Your Character card in front of you — who or what you're playing right now.
- In the center: the Stage — three slots for Scene cards. This is the shared clinical reality: the patient's current state, the active pathology, the treatment in progress.
- Next to it: the Quest card + Quest Die (starts at 1).

| State | Rule |
|---|---|
| Empty slot | Fill it with a Scene card. |
| Stage full (3/3) | Overwrite a slot. Reframe what was there — yes-and, not yes-but-actually-no. |

### Character Switch

You can play a Character card into your own slot at any time. Your old character either leaves the case or becomes an NPC controlled by the table. Change your voice and posture immediately.

## The Quest Die

The Quest Die tracks the progression of the clinical case. You can modify this template to fit your own Medicine Hero's Journey.

| Die Value | Phase | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | **Anamnesis** | Symptoms appear. Characters introduce themselves. The clinical picture begins to take shape. |
| 3–4 | **Workup** | Investigations ordered. Pathophysiology escalates. Something unexpected emerges. |
| 5 | **Crisis Point** | The darkest moment. The differential narrows. Something must give. |
| 6 | **Grand Finale** | Resolution. The mechanism is named. The treatment chosen. The case closes — elegantly or catastrophically. |

> This is orientation, not obligation. A case that goes Comic → Tragic → Mechanism Flash in three turns is fine. Clinical reality rarely follows the textbook arc.

### Moving the Die

- **+2** — Hard push. The case is accelerating. Use sparingly.
- **+1** — Normal progression. The clinical picture develops.
- **0** — Hold. Let the pathophysiology breathe.
- **–1** — Step back. A complication reframes everything.

> **NOTE:** Quest Die never goes below 1. At 6: Finale is triggered next turn.

### Quest Collapse — The Grand Finale

When the Quest Die hits 6, the active player tells the Finale on their next turn. Normal story. The Quest must resolve. It must land.

1. **Memory** — Quest card becomes a record. Place it face-down to the side; write the mechanism on it if running the Mechanism Log.
2. **Tabula Rasa** — Stage clears. All Scene cards to discard. (Or use Staged Clearing — see below.)
3. **New Quest** — Next player runs Quest Reveal. Quest Die to 1.

> *"The Finale doesn't have to be a textbook answer. It has to be true to what everyone built. A messy ending that was earned beats a clean answer that was imported from the index."*

### Optional Rules

- **Quest Fail** — If the Quest Die falls below 1, the case collapses without resolution. No Finale. The patient outcome is ambiguous. The next player runs Quest Reveal immediately. The collapse is the story — medicine doesn't always resolve cleanly.
- **Staged Clearing** — Roll D6 after the Finale. 1–2: remove 1 Scene card. 3–4: remove 2. 5–6: remove all. Surviving cards carry their history into the new case.

## Social Calls

After a story — before the next player starts — anyone can call one of these. Your Quest Die move stands unless Blödsinn! lands.

| Call | Effect | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **KayfaBINGO!** | That landed. | The mechanism clicked. Spontaneous — no explanation needed. Pure praise. |
| **KayfaBOGGLE?** | I'm lost. | One concrete question — not a judgment, a gap. Active player addresses it in 1–2 sentences. Play continues. |
| **KayfaBONGO!** | Power as calculation. | Someone resolved a Power as a stat ("I reduce creatinine by 40%") instead of a story beat — call it. They restate as story, not transaction. |
| **BLÖDSINN!** | Broke Kayfabe. | A rule was broken: out of character · Scene card forgotten · Mode ignored. Majority decides. If it stands: Quest Die does not move. |

> *"KayfaBOGGLE is not a critique. It's a clinical question. Keep it short. Keep it about the mechanism. Keep it kind. KayfaBONGO is not a penalty — it's a redirect from calculation back to story."*

> *"Professor Kayfabian (Optional): If calls are causing arguments, one player takes the Professor Kayfabian role for one story — final call on any dispute. Passes left. No permanent power. Most tables won't need this."*

### Table Culture — Highly Recommended

- **Kayfab-Finger Rule** — Point at each card as you mention it. Helps beginners and keeps the Stage oriented.
- **No Shaming** — Mode 5 (Teaching Round) is not pimping. Gaps in knowledge are the content of the game, not the failure condition.
- **Silence Is Allowed** — Thinking out loud is a valid story move.
- **Overacting Is Encouraged** — The more committed the Intro Ritual, the better the mechanism sticks. This is not a coincidence.
- **Laughter Breaks Nothing** — If everyone's laughing, the mechanism is landing.

## Powers & Cards

*Nothing on a card is a rule. Everything is an invitation. Powers are narrative invitations — not separate mechanics, not damage modifiers.*

### The Anatomy of a Card

- **Image** — Makes a claim without words. Starting point for any story.
- **LORE** — The mechanism or clinical context. Use it or invent from the image.
- **POWER** — What this entity does in the clinical story. Mandatory when introduced. Perform it — don't calculate it.

### How to Use a Power

When you introduce a card, read the Power aloud in the Intro Ritual. Then show us what it looks like when that Power comes alive in the clinical story.

| Don't say | Say instead |
|---|---|
| "I block COX-1 and reduce thromboxane by 70%." | "Every platelet in the bloodstream goes suddenly, permanently quiet. The surgical team will notice this tomorrow. The patient's wife is on warfarin. Nobody checked." |

### Quest Formats

Three options — use whichever fits your session:

- **Standard Quest** — Any card can become a Quest. Starting player frames the clinical tension from the card's name and image.
- **Prepared Quests** — Write clinical scenarios on blank cards before playing: *"Diagnose the cause of this patient's hyponatraemia before the consultant round."*
- **Mechanism Quests (advanced)** — The Quest is a mechanism to reconstruct collectively: *"Explain why loop diuretics cause hypomagnesaemia."* / *"Build the complement cascade from C3 to MAC — one card, one step."*

## Solo Play — Study Session Mode

MedKayfab works solo. Use it for pre-exam preparation, rotation review, or reconstructing a clinical encounter from your day.

**Solo setup:** Draw 5 cards. Pick one as your Character. Draw a Quest card or write your own. Stage is empty. Quest Die to 1.

**Solo play:** Play all Characters yourself, switching perspectives. The Story-Mode Die still applies. After each story, write one sentence in the Mechanism Log.

**Solo Lore-Flash rule:** On a roll of 3, cover the LORE text with your hand. Recite it from memory. Then uncover and check. No penalty for being wrong — adjust and move on.

> *"Solo MedKayfab is essentially the Feynman Technique with cards and a die. You pick a concept, you explain it out loud as simply and completely as you can, you find the gaps, you go back. The cards make the gaps visible."*

## Learning Add-Ons (Optional)

These are optional layers. They make the game more educational at the cost of some narrative momentum. Use them when the group wants to study harder, not just play.

### Add-On A — Anki Mode

After each Finale, each player selects one card from the session and writes: Front = character name + one clinical clue. Back = core mechanism in one sentence. Add to your Anki deck. Review tomorrow.

> *"MedKayfab surfaces the card. Anki makes it stick. They're not competing — they're the same memory cycle at different timescales."*

### Add-On B — Feynman Round

Once per Quest (usually at Quest Die 4), one player picks any card on the Stage and explains its mechanism to the group as if they have no medical background. No jargon. No acronyms. One minute maximum. If the group understands it: Quest Die +1 bonus.

> *"If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet. Feynman said that. He was right. Doc FrizzleBob agrees — reluctantly, because agreeing with physicists feels like admitting defeat."*

### Add-On C — Spaced Repetition Challenge

Before dealing, shuffle in 3–5 cards from a previous session's Mechanism Log topics, marked with a small dot on the back. When a marked card appears: the player who introduced it last time must explain its mechanism from memory before the Intro Ritual. If they can: +1 Quest Die. If they can't: the card still plays, but the gap is logged.

### Add-On D — Peer Teaching Round

After each Quest Finale, one player is randomly chosen (roll D6 — highest = teacher). That player takes one card and explains its mechanism and clinical relevance. Maximum 3 minutes. The group can ask one question each. The teacher does not need to know the answer perfectly.

> *"The best way to learn something is to teach it. The best way to teach it is to not be afraid of the gap. In this game, the gap is the interesting part."*
