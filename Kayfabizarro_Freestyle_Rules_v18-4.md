# **Kayfabizarro Freestyle Rules**

**The Social Story Wrestling Card Game** *by Georg von Westphalen*

Freestyle Mode · 1–6 Players · 20–60 Min · v18.1

| Term | Meaning |
| ----- | ----- |
| **Kayfabe** | Wrestling-speak for "the fiction is real." What happens in the ring is performed, but the crowd believes it. At this table: your story is the reality. Commit to it. |
| **Bizarro** | In comics and fiction, the mirror-world where logic runs backwards and nonsense makes sense. Here: the permission to be strange, wrong, and surprising on purpose. |

**Welcome To Uncle FrizzleBob's Komik Kard Kult\!**

You're about to make up a story together — using Kayfabizarro cards, two dice, and whatever's in your head.

One sentence: everyone takes turns introducing a card and telling a short story, until the story lands.

Read up to Setup. Then start. The rest makes sense as you play.

*Stay fluffy.* — Uncle FrizzleBob (Professional Reality-Wrestler & Epistemiologic Outlaw)

## **1\. What Happens in This Game**

You each play a **Character**. Together you build a shared **Stage** of cards and push a shared **Quest** toward its ending.

On your turn: roll to find out what kind of story you're telling, introduce a card with a short ritual, and tell a short story in character.

The **Quest Die** tracks how close the story is to its ending — but it's not a timer. You move it. You decide when to push hard or let things breathe.

When the Quest Die reaches 6, someone tells the **Finale**. Quest resolved. New episode.

No win condition. No score. Just a story that didn't exist before you sat down — and a sense of *whose stories landed*.

One Quest \= about 20–30 minutes. Three Quests fills an evening. Stop when it feels done.

## **2\. What You Need**

* **Kayfabizarro cards** — Designed for the Kayfabizarro deck. Any illustrated cards work — tarot, cut-up comics, playing cards with images. Each player brings 15–20 cards. Shuffle everything into one shared face-down deck. Every card can be played as Character, Scene, or Quest.  
* **Two D6** — One Story-Mode Die, one Quest Die. Both stay visible on the table at all times.  
* **1–6 people** — And a table. Optional: a big sheet of paper for doodling as the story grows.

*"No board. No pawns. No manual thicker than your wrist. That was a design decision, not laziness. Probably."*

## **3\. The Six Story Modes**

At the start of your turn, roll the Story-Mode Die. This is your **Story Mode** for this turn. Not optional. Commit to it.

| D6 | Mode | Play it like… |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| 1 | **Tragic** | *Loss is real. Push forward anyway.* |
| 2 | **Comic** | *Things go wrong. It's your fault. Play the gap.* |
| 3 | **Absurd** | *The impossible is normal. Don't explain it.* |
| 4 | **Heroic** | *Your Character is right for this moment. Play the peak.* |
| 5 | **Mystical** | *Something's happening beneath the surface.* |
| 6 | **Forbidden** | *Cross the line the story's been avoiding. Sell it.* |

The Mode is a frame, not a cage. If it creates a contradiction with your story — that *is* the story. Play the contradiction.

*"Forbidden doesn't mean offensive. It means your Character walks through the door the story has been politely locking. Make the table uncomfortable in a way that serves the story. If someone's genuinely uncomfortable: stop, check in, use KayfaBOGGLE or just talk. The story serves the people, not the other way around."*

## **4\. The Intro Ritual — Perform Reality**

Every card that enters play — Character, Scene card, or Quest — gets the same short ritual. Think: wrestling entrance. The spotlight hits. You walk out and you *are* whoever the card says you are.

**Three beats. Thirty seconds. In character.**

| 1 | Name it — Announce the card's name. Loud. This is who or what just entered. |
| :---- | :---- |
| 2 | **Claim it** — One sentence: what does this card want? What is its truth? Read it off the card or invent it from the image. |
| 3 | **Power it** — **Don't explain it. Perform it.** Show us what this Power does to reality *right now*. |

*"The Intro isn't a character sheet. It's a promise. Say it like you mean it — the story starts the moment you commit.*

*And yes — every card, every time. Quest cards, Scene cards, new Characters. Always. It's what keeps everyone in the same story."*

## **5\. Your Play Area — 3 Scenes of Reality**

* Your **Character card** in front of you — who you're playing right now.  
* In the center: the **Stage** — three slots for Scene cards. This is the shared reality everyone's story is happening inside.  
* Next to it: the **Quest card** \+ **Quest Die** (starts at 1).

## **6\. Setup — 2 Minute Quick Start**

| 1 | Deal — Give 3 cards to each player. Keep them secret. |
| :---- | :---- |
| 2 | **Characters** — Everyone picks 1 card, places it face-up. Run the Intro Ritual — in character, out loud. |
| 3 | **Quest Reveal** — Starting player draws a Quest card, places it next to the Stage, runs the Intro Ritual, frames the tension. Quest Die to 1\. |
| 4 | **Go** — Stage is empty. Starting player tells the first story. |

**New to this?** Skip the Story-Mode Die for the first round of introductions — everyone just runs the Intro Ritual for their Character. Once everyone's in, roll as normal.

*"You will feel slightly ridiculous for the first few minutes. That's the point. The ridiculous is where the story lives."*

## **7\. Your Turn — 5 Steps of Story Wrestling**

Every turn follows the same steps. Always in this order.

| 1 | Draw — Draw 1 card (temporarily 4 hand cards). |
| :---- | :---- |
| 2 | **Roll** — Roll the Story-Mode Die. This is your Story Mode. |
| 3 | **Play** — Choose one card from your hand (3 hand cards remaining): play it as a **Scene card** onto the Stage — or play it as your **new Character** (Character Switch). Either way: run the Intro Ritual, then tell your story. |
| 4 | **Tell** — Tell a story in character, in your Mode. Build on the vibe and ideas of the card you just played — and weave in the other Scene cards additively. The Quest must be *felt*. If you performed the Power: card goes to discard. |
| 5 | **Move** — Quest Die: **\+2** (hard push) / **\+1** (advance) / **0** (hold) / **–1** (step back). Your dramaturgical decision. Quest Die never goes below 1\. At 6: Finale next turn. |

*"Why would you ever choose –1? When a twist reframes everything, or when the next player needs room to build. The Quest Die is yours to conduct — not just push forward."*

### 

### **The Quest Die — A Dramaturgical Guide**

The Quest Die is a story compass, not just a countdown. As it rises, the dramatic weight shifts.

| Die | Story phase |
| ----- | ----- |
| 1–2 | **Akt I** — Intro. Establish the world, introduce Characters, set the scene. |
| 3–4 | **Akt II** — Conflict. Things escalate, something breaks. |
| 5 | **Ordeal** — The darkest moment. Something must give. |
| 6 | **Finale** — Resolution. Return with the elixir, whatever form that takes. |

This is orientation, not obligation. A story that goes Absurd → Tragic → Heroic in three turns is fine. The Hero's Journey is a suggestion, not a cage.

### **The Stage — 3 Scene Slots**

The Stage holds three Scene cards — the shared reality. As scenes get added or replaced, the reality shifts. Nothing gets erased — just reframed.

| State | Rule |
| ----- | ----- |
| Empty slot | Fill it with a Scene card. |
| Stage full (3/3) | Overwrite a slot. Re-frame what was there — yes-and, not yes-but-actually-no. |

## **8\. Social Feedback — Did You Sell It?**

After a story — before the next player starts — anyone can call one of these:

| Call | When to use |
| ----- | ----- |
| KayfaBINGO | That landed. Spontaneous, no explanation needed. |
| KayfaBONGO | If someone resolves a Power like a mechanic ("I deal 3 damage"), anyone can say it. The player restates as story, not transaction. |
| KayfaBOGGLE | One concrete question — not a judgment, a gap. The active player addresses it in 1–2 sentences. Play continues. |
| Blödsinn\! | A rule was broken: out of character · Scene card forgotten · Mode ignored. Majority decides. If it stands: Quest Die does not move this turn. |

Your Quest Die move stands unless Blödsinn\! lands. If nobody calls anything: game continues with your declared move.

*"KayfaBOGGLE is not a criticism. It's a question. Keep it short, keep it about the story, keep it kind. KayfaBONGO is not a penalty — it's a redirect."*

### **King Kayfabian (Optional)**

If calls are causing arguments, one player takes the King Kayfabian role for one story — final call on any dispute. Then it passes left. No permanent power. Most tables won't need this.

## **9\. Powers — Perform, Don't Calculate**

Many Kayfabizarro cards have a **Power** printed on them.

**Nothing on a card is a rule. Everything is an invitation.** Powers are narrative invitations — not separate mechanics, not modifiers, not exceptions. No card overrides this principle.

When you introduce a card, read the Power aloud in the Intro Ritual. Then show us what it looks like when that Power comes alive in the story.

**Don't say:** *"I deal 3 damage to you."* **Say:** *"Your empire glitches. The generals forget what war is. Seven kinds of collapse happen at once."*

If you wove the Power into your story, the card goes to discard after the story. Otherwise it stays on the Stage until overwritten.

*"The Power is a creative constraint. You are not calculating outcomes — you are performing possibilities."*

## **10\. Character Switch — Voluntary Drama**

Your Character can be switched at any point — but only if you choose it. No mechanical trigger.

Want your Character to die? Play the death as part of your story. Then immediately choose one card from your hand as your new Character, run the Intro Ritual, draw back up to 3 cards.

*"A good death scene is a gift to the story."*

## **11\. Quest Collapse — The Episode Finale**

When the Quest Die hits 6, the episode ends. The active player tells the Finale on their next turn — same 5 steps, but the Quest *must* resolve. Tragic, heroic, absurd — the central tension has to be addressed.

| 1 | Memory — Quest card stays on the table as a shared memory of this episode. |
| :---- | :---- |
| 2 | **Clear** — Stage is cleared — tabula rasa. All Scene cards to discard. *(See Staged Clearing below for an alternative.)* |
| 3 | **New Quest** — Next player draws a card and plays it with the full Quest Reveal ritual. |
| 4 | **Reset** — Quest Die back to 1\. New episode begins. |

**Quest Fail (Optional):** If the Quest Die is moved below 1, the Quest fails without resolution. Discard it. Next player runs Quest Reveal immediately. No Finale, no memory. The collapse *is* the story.

**Staged Clearing (Optional):** Instead of full tabula rasa, roll the Story-Mode Die after the Finale. **1–2:** remove 1 Scene card. **3–4:** remove 2\. **5–6:** all three. Surviving cards carry their history into the new episode.

*"The Finale doesn't have to be good. It has to be true — true to what everyone built. A messy ending that was earned beats a neat ending that was imported."*

## **12\. All Game Rituals at a Glance**

| Ritual | When | How |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Intro Ritual** | Every card entering play | Name it · Claim it · Power it (perform it) · 30 seconds · in character |
| **Quest Reveal** | Episode start · after every Quest collapse | Intro Ritual → frame the tension → Quest Die to 1 → first story |
| **Character Switch** | Instead of playing a Scene card | Intro Ritual for new Character → brief exit for old Character → Stage unchanged |
| **Quest Collapse** | Quest Die hits 6 | Finale story → Quest card as memory → tabula rasa → Quest Reveal → Die to 1 |

## **14\. Common Kayfab Questions**

**Do I have to mention every Scene card?** Yes — at least acknowledge each one. A single word is enough. Recognition, not analysis.

**What if my story doesn't make sense?** Perfect. Coherence is optional. Connection matters more than logic.

**Can I lie?** Yes. Cards don't enforce truth. They enforce claims.

**What if the Powers look like rules?** They aren't. See *The Only Rule That Matters*. Perform, don't calculate.

**What if no one calls Blödsinn\! but I know someone forgot a card?** Then you call it. Or you don't. Both are valid.

**Can I pass?** Yes. Passing too often kills momentum — use it sparingly.

**What if the Quest Die keeps shifting and everything turns chaotic?** That's working as intended. Chaos is a valid game state.

**Can I add my own cards?** Absolutely. Blank cards exist for this reason. Make them strange.

**What if several people call something at the same time?** All calls are heard. BINGO and BOGGLE can stack. If BINGO and Blödsinn\! clash: Blödsinn\! takes priority.

**Is there a correct way to play?** No. Only the rules that emerge at your table. Kayfabizarro adapts to the group — not the other way around.

**Can I play solo?** Yes — there's a Solo add-on. Without it: play all Characters yourself, alternating perspectives. Works well as a journaling tool.

## **15\. Table Culture — Non-Negotiable**

**Kayfab-Finger Rule:** Point at each card as you mention it. Helps beginners, kids, and keeps the table oriented.

**Tempo Matters:** Fast play \= chaotic fun. Slow play \= emotional depth. Both are valid.

**Silence Is Allowed:** Listening can be a move.

**Overacting Is Encouraged:** Big gestures, strange voices, exaggerated drama — go for it.

**Laughter Breaks Nothing:** If everyone's laughing, you're playing it right.

**Forbidden makes someone uncomfortable?** Check in. Use KayfaBOGGLE or just talk. The story serves the people, not the other way around.

## **16\. Solo Play**

Play all Characters yourself, switching perspectives. The Story-Mode Die still applies. The Hero's Journey arc on the Quest Die is especially useful — you'll know where you are in the story.

Without other players: after each story, write one sentence about what shifted. That's your Blödsinn\! and your BINGO in one.
